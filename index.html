Traditional WordPress, not headless · honest tradeoff math

WordPress, not headless.

Your RFP prefers WordPress and explicitly invites a headless vs. traditional rationale. For Rick Mitchell's two-person team running this site for five years, traditional wins on every axis that matters. The largest talent pool, the most mature plugin ecosystem, the lowest total cost of ownership. We are not upselling an architecture your team cannot sustainably operate.

Why WordPress
Editorial usability for a small team.
Rick's team publishes content, not support tickets. Gutenberg real-time preview, in-context editing, media library. The benchmark for content author experience, especially after WordPress 6.x.
Why WordPress
Largest developer ecosystem.
When Holy Family needs a developer in three years, WordPress talent is abundant and competitively priced. Drupal talent is smaller and more expensive. Across five years that difference compounds into real dollars.
Why not headless
Editorial workflow degrades.
Real-time preview and in-context editing require expensive re-engineering. Yoast, Gravity Forms, GTM integration all assume a traditional front-end. Two codebases to maintain. Permanent React/Next.js skills dependency.
Future proof
REST API ready.
ACF Pro exposes every post type via REST from day one. If HFU ever needs a mobile app, digital signage, or a decoupled front end in the future, the content layer is already headless-ready. You pay for that flexibility only when you need it.
