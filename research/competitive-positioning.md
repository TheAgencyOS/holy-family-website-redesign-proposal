# Competitive Positioning Brief

**Prepared for:** Holy Family University Website Redesign RFP
**Date:** April 2026
**Classification:** Internal strategy supplement (optional appendix)

---

## 1. The Competitive Landscape

Holy Family University's RFP will attract interest from several established higher education web agencies. Based on the project scope -- a 1,300-page Drupal-to-WordPress migration with full redesign, WCAG 2.2 AA compliance, and Slate CRM integration -- the likely respondents include OHO Interactive (Boston, 100+ employees, dominant in Ivy League and top-100 university redesigns), Promet Source (Chicago, mid-size Drupal agency with a dedicated higher ed practice and their Provus EDU product), Kanopi Studios (San Francisco/distributed, 70+ specialists, known for phased delivery and long-term retainer relationships), and Electric Citizen (Minneapolis, smaller Drupal shop with an accessibility-forward reputation). Regional Philadelphia firms such as Bluecadet, Alloy, and O3 World may also respond, though their higher education portfolios are thinner.

Each of these firms brings legitimate strengths. They would not have survived in this market otherwise. But each also carries a structural misalignment with what Holy Family actually needs. OHO Interactive is built to serve large research universities with six- and seven-figure digital budgets. Their published guidance prices university redesigns at $500K and up, and their team model is optimized for institutions with dedicated 5-10 person web departments. For a 3,700-student private university with a lean two-person digital team, the risk is not that OHO would do poor work -- it is that Holy Family's project becomes a mid-tier account staffed by junior team members while the firm's senior talent focuses on flagship clients. Promet Source presents a different problem. They are a Drupal-first agency whose Provus EDU platform is built on Drupal, not WordPress. Holy Family's RFP expresses a preference for WordPress. Promet's most natural response will be to argue against that preference and redirect the university back to Drupal -- which serves Promet's business model but not Holy Family's stated goals or the long-term maintainability needs of Rick Mitchell's team.

Kanopi Studios is perhaps the most polished competitor, with strong case studies and a proven phased delivery model. Their vulnerability is philosophical, not technical. Kanopi's "continuous improvement" engagement model is designed to create an ongoing dependency relationship where the university pays monthly retainers indefinitely for changes that a well-trained internal team should be able to make independently. For a university that needs to own and operate its website for 5+ years with a small internal staff, the Kanopi model optimizes for agency revenue, not institutional self-sufficiency. Electric Citizen, while capable on accessibility and mid-size university projects, is a small Drupal shop based in Minneapolis. They lack demonstrated experience with private Catholic institutions, and their CMS expertise centers on Drupal rather than WordPress. On a WordPress-preferred RFP, they face the same structural disadvantage as Promet -- either they work outside their core platform or they spend proposal space arguing against the university's stated direction.

---

## 2. Our Structural Advantages

- **WordPress is our primary platform, not a secondary offering.** Promet Source and Electric Citizen are Drupal agencies. If Holy Family selects a Drupal shop for a WordPress project, the university either gets a team working outside its deepest expertise or gets talked into a platform change that does not serve its long-term staffing model. We build WordPress sites as our core business. Rick Mitchell's team will inherit a codebase built by people who think in WordPress natively -- custom themes, ACF field groups, Gutenberg blocks, WP-CLI workflows, and Pantheon-native deployment patterns.

- **We are appropriately sized for this engagement.** OHO Interactive fields 100+ employees and prices accordingly. Holy Family does not need a 100-person agency -- it needs a focused team where senior practitioners do the work, not delegate it. Our team structure means the people who present in the pitch are the same people who write the code, design the templates, and run the content migration. There is no handoff to a junior bench after the contract is signed.

- **We bring private Catholic university understanding that no named competitor claims.** OHO's portfolio skews toward large research universities and Ivy League institutions. Promet, Kanopi, and Electric Citizen serve higher education broadly but have no visible specialization in private Catholic institutions. Holy Family's website must do something these competitors' standard playbooks do not account for: integrate Catholic mission, campus ministry, and faith-based identity alongside rigorous academic program marketing, enrollment conversion funnels, and modern UX. That integration requires more than applying a template -- it requires understanding how these elements coexist and reinforce each other in the minds of prospective students and their families.

- **We build for ownership, not dependency.** Our engagement model is designed around a knowledge transfer plan with named training sessions, documented outcomes, and a clear definition of what Rick Mitchell's team can do independently after launch. Where Kanopi's continuous improvement model keeps the agency as an indefinite intermediary, our approach treats post-launch independence as a primary deliverable. The component library, content governance documentation, and training curriculum we deliver are designed so that routine content updates, new program pages, and template extensions happen in-house -- without a monthly retainer call.

## 3. Why This Matters to Holy Family

This is not an abstract competitive exercise. The practical consequence of partner selection is that Rick Mitchell, his Senior Web Strategist, and future team members will live with this website for five or more years after launch. Every architectural decision, every component pattern, every content model choice will either empower that team or constrain it. A Drupal agency building in WordPress will produce code that reflects Drupal thinking in a WordPress shell -- workable, but never native, and harder for a WordPress-skilled team to extend and maintain. An oversized agency will deliver a polished product but leave behind a codebase built for the agency's workflow, not the university's. A dependency-model agency will deliver a site that works beautifully as long as the retainer checks clear, but creates risk the moment the university needs to redirect that budget elsewhere.

Holy Family needs a partner who understands three things simultaneously: modern web technology and WordPress architecture at a professional level; the enrollment marketing reality of a private university competing for students in a declining-enrollment market; and the distinctive identity of a Catholic institution where mission is not a sidebar but a foundational element of the brand. Katherine Primus's marketing vision, Mark Green's technology governance requirements, and Edward Wright's enrollment conversion goals all need to be served by the same website. The partner who builds it should be small enough to care deeply about this specific project, expert enough in WordPress to build it natively, and grounded enough in private Catholic higher education to get the nuance right without being taught.

## 4. The Bottom Line

We are not the largest agency responding to this RFP, and we are not the cheapest. We are the one whose platform expertise, team model, institutional understanding, and engagement philosophy align most directly with what Holy Family University actually needs: a WordPress-native website built for long-term ownership by a small internal team, designed to convert prospective students while honoring the university's Catholic mission, and delivered by senior practitioners who treat this project as a priority rather than a line item. That is what we propose to build, and that is what we will deliver.
