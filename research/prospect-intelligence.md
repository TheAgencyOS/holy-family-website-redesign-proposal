# Holy Family University — Prospect Intelligence Brief

**RFP:** Website Redesign | Due April 17, 2026 | Proposal Due: 7 days
**Prospect Stage:** Qualified | **Confidence Level:** B+ | **Estimated Deal Size:** $150K–$350K

---

## Executive Summary

Holy Family is a financially healthy, small Catholic university ($96M revenue, $30M endowment, S&P A-minus) with a brand-new VP of Marketing (Katherine Primus, March 2025) and a technically capable director of digital experience (Rick Mitchell, April 2024). They are running an outdated Drupal site (1,300 pages) and have already completed internal preparation work (content audits, stakeholder interviews, brand guidelines). The combination of institutional financial stability, new leadership wanting to make their mark, and existing technical preparation makes this a strong prospect — but at a modest budget for a 6–12 month project.

## Budget & Comparable Pricing

### Institutional Budget Context
- **Annual Operating Budget:** $91.7M
- **Marketing/Communications Spend:** Typically 2–4% of operating budget = ~$1.8M–$3.7M annually
- **This Project:** Capital/project expense, separate from annual marketing budget

### Comparable Project Pricing (Higher Ed Industry)
- **Typical scope for a 1,300-page redesign:** 3,000–5,000 hours of work
- **Blended rates (mid-tier agencies):** $150–200/hr
- **Industry pricing range:** $450K–$1M for premium agencies (OHO, Promet)

### Holy Family's Realistic Budget
- **Estimated budget range:** $150K–$350K for full execution
- **Why the lower range?** Small endowment ($30M vs. large universities' $1B+), modest enrollment, tight marketing budget, plus institutional preference for efficiency
- **Possible structure:** Could be split across FY2026 and FY2027 (e.g., $100K for discovery/design in FY26, $150K for development in FY27)

### Budget Authority Signal
- VP Marketing hired March 2025 (new, wants to make her mark)
- Director of Digital hired April 2024 (onboarded, ready to lead projects)
- This suggests **money is already earmarked** for a digital initiative. The budget exists; the question is allocation.

**Implication for proposal:** Price competitively ($180K–$280K for a complete 6-month build), but don't leave money on the table by bidding too low. Positioning around value (content migration, analytics setup, knowledge transfer) is more important than race-to-bottom pricing.

## Pain Points & Leverage Points

### What They Explicitly Complained About (from RFP)
1. **Outdated design** → "Doesn't reflect vibrant campus culture"
2. **Complex navigation** → "Overly complex, users can't find things"
3. **Inconsistent content** → "No governance, no templates, looks different everywhere"
4. **Gaps in mobile optimization** → "Not built for phones"
5. **Accessibility issues** → "WCAG 2.2 AA is mandatory"
6. **Enrollment-driven experience missing** → "Prospective students don't convert"

### What They're Implicitly Complaining About
- **Content debt:** 1,300 pages that have grown organically without governance. 20–40% are likely obsolete or redundant.
- **No content model:** No structured templates, no field definitions, no reusable patterns → leads to inconsistency
- **Drupal version debt:** If on D7 or D9 (likely), they're running EOL software — security and upgrade pressure is real
- **No design system:** Each department built their own page styles; no component library to enforce consistency
- **Team capability gaps:** Current internal team may be strong on content and governance but lacking on modern front-end development and design systems

### Your Negotiation Levers

| Lever | How to Use It |
|---|---|
| **Content consolidation** | Propose content audit upfront. Offer to reduce 1,300 → 800 active pages. Saves migration cost + improves UX. Frame as "content strategy," not "cutting corners." |
| **Phased delivery** | Launch Phase 1 (homepage, program pages, admissions flow, faculty directory) in 4 months. Phase 2 (remaining content migration) in months 5–6. De-risks timeline, spreads cost, shows early wins. |
| **Design system extension** | Mitchell already maintains a design system. Don't replace it; upgrade and document it. Propose handing off a living component library + Figma kit that his team can extend. This is a value proposition that resonates with resource-constrained teams. |
| **Analytics continuity** | They already have GA4, GTM, Crazy Egg, Hotjar. Don't rip and replace. Propose preserving and enhancing their stack. Shows respect for existing work; reduces transition risk. |
| **Post-launch support model** | Rather than a 1-time delivery, position ongoing support as a retainer (e.g., $2K–$3K/month for 12 months post-launch). This shifts the relationship from transactional to partnership and creates recurring revenue. |

## Critical Open Questions

These should be asked during the Q&A period (deadline: March 27, but you still have time to submit before April 17):

1. **What version of Drupal is the current site running?** (This determines migration complexity and security urgency.)
2. **Is the university currently hosted on Pantheon?** (If yes, WordPress migration is simpler; if not, adds hosting transition work.)
3. **What is the explicit budget range for this project?** (Helps you position your proposal at the right price point.)
4. **What integrations are required beyond Slate?** (SIS, LMS, CRM, course catalog? Each is a scope/cost multiplier.)
5. **Who else is on the selection committee besides Rick Mitchell?** (Need to understand all stakeholder priorities.)
6. **Is post-launch support/hosting included in the base scope or a separate engagement?** (Hosting can be recurring revenue.)
7. **What is the current site hosting arrangement?** (Pantheon, Acquia, self-hosted? Affects migration and ongoing cost.)

**Recommended approach:** Submit 2–3 of the most important questions in the Q&A period, then use the answers to refine your proposal strategy.

## Recommendation: Should You Bid?

### Yes, bid this RFP. Here's why:

**Pros:**
- ✅ **Financially qualified:** S&P A-minus, profitable, no distress signals
- ✅ **Budget likely available:** Money already earmarked; leadership in place to allocate it
- ✅ **Technical fit:** WordPress migration (your strength) aligns with their CMS direction
- ✅ **Decision-maker alignment:** Rick Mitchell speaks your language (technical, component-based design); Primus wants a win; Green will value your technical rigor
- ✅ **Realistic timeline:** June kickoff → Spring 2027 launch is achievable with disciplined scope
- ✅ **Differentiation possible:** Your AI-assisted migration + content consolidation expertise is a legitimate edge vs. OHO/Promet

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ **Budget ceiling $350K:** Tight if you're a premium-priced shop; doable if you're lean and efficient
- ⚠️ **Competitive field is strong:** OHO, Promet, Kanopi are bidding; you'll need a crisp differentiation story
- ⚠️ **HE portfolio requirement:** They want 3–5 university case studies. If you don't have them, you're fighting uphill. (Note: You said you work for a higher ed agency — assuming you have institutional case studies you can reference.)
- ⚠️ **7-day turnaround:** April 17 deadline is tight. Proposal will need to be crisp and focused, not comprehensive.

### Bid strategy (if you proceed):

1. **Lead with WordPress migration expertise.** Frame Drupal → WordPress as the right move for their team size and skills.
2. **Propose content consolidation as Phase 1 deliverable.** Show that you're thinking about reducing scope, not gold-plating it.
3. **Price aggressively but confidently.** Propose $220K–$280K for full 6-month build with post-launch support as optional add-on.
4. **Emphasize collaboration over dictation.** Rick Mitchell is technical; he will want a partner, not a vendor. Position your approach as "extending your design system" and "transferring knowledge to your team."
5. **Design for handoff.** Mitchell's team will maintain this site for 5+ years post-launch. Every deliverable should be documented for their eventual ownership.
