Proposal / Case studies / 01 Truman State
Case study 01 · Enrollment strategy & competitive repositioning
Client institution Truman State University Public liberal arts · Kirksville, Missouri · ~4,000 students

Competing inside a crowded public system, with clarity instead of spend.

Truman faced the same structural pressure Holy Family faces: a mission-distinct institution working inside a competitive landscape dominated by larger, better-resourced peers. The engagement focused on sharper positioning, funnel alignment, and disciplined execution inside an existing budget. The same model we propose for Holy Family.

The challenge

Recruiting against the University of Missouri system, on a regional budget.

Truman operates inside a competitive Missouri public landscape where the University of Missouri institutions carry stronger name recognition, broader program portfolios, and larger marketing budgets. That combination produced brand-perception pressure, yield pressure, and enrollment-growth constraints inside a real budget envelope. The institution did not need louder marketing; it needed sharper strategy and a funnel that converted.

The engagement

Funnel assessment, positioning review, messaging refinement, CRM alignment.

The work began with a full enrollment-funnel assessment and a review of Truman's brand positioning against the Missouri public landscape. From there the engagement produced competitive differentiators, a refined messaging architecture, a recruitment strategy aligned to institutional strengths, and a set of conversion improvements made inside Truman's existing CRM environment.

  • Enrollment funnel assessment across inquiry, application, admit, and deposit stages.
  • Brand positioning review against the Missouri public university landscape.
  • Competitive differentiator identification, tested against the recruitment audience.
  • Messaging architecture refinement and alignment across channels.
  • Recruitment strategy rebuilt around institutional strengths rather than generic "best value" framing.
  • Conversion work inside Truman's existing CRM environment, with no platform migration required.
Our role

Scott Novak led directly, alongside Truman's admissions leadership.

Small College Consulting served as strategic enrollment advisor. Scott Novak led the engagement directly, working with Truman's admissions leadership rather than replacing it. The team did not swap platforms, replace staff, or introduce expensive new tools. The improvements came from strategic clarity and disciplined execution inside existing capacity.

The outcome

Stronger differentiation, tighter marketing-to-enrollment alignment.

Measured outcomes

Strengthened competitive differentiation against larger Missouri public peers, with messaging architecture tested against recruitment audiences rather than internal preferences.

Tighter alignment between marketing activity and enrollment KPIs, with recruitment campaigns aligned to the admissions funnel rather than run as standalone brand exercises.

Performance improvement delivered inside the existing budget envelope, with no net increase in marketing spend and no vendor dependency introduced. Specific metric, to be released by Truman
