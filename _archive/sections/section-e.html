E.5

Why this investment is defensible

1. Platform fit

WordPress expertise, not a platform pivot

We build in WordPress daily. Several likely respondents to this RFP are Drupal shops operating outside their core platform expertise, either pushing back on the WordPress decision or retraining their senior team on your budget. You are paying for a team that already knows the Gutenberg, ACF, Gravity Forms, and Pantheon ecosystem at a senior level, not for their WordPress learning curve.

2. Cost structure

AI-accelerated migration, higher quality at lower cost

AI handles the repetitive 80 percent of migration work across five specific stages: content audit, schema mapping, content transformation, semantic redirect mapping, and post-migration QA. Human experts spend their hours on the strategic 20 percent that requires judgment. This is not AI replacing humans; it is AI compressing the labor-intensive work that inflates cost at traditional agencies.

3. Total cost of ownership

Knowledge transfer reduces ongoing costs

We sell self-sufficiency, not dependency. The deliverables (living design system, documented Gutenberg blocks, governance playbook, recorded training, developer docs) are designed so that Rick Mitchell's team can operate, extend, and maintain the site independently after launch. Your total cost of ownership over five years is materially lower than with agencies whose business model depends on you needing them forever.

4. Enrollment impact

Measurable outcomes, not a cosmetic redesign

Industry benchmarks for well-executed higher ed redesigns show 25 to 35 percent improvement in RFI submissions and application starts within 12 months. Standout cases: CityU of Seattle +171 percent RFI in month one, Harper College +962 percent applications with personalization, Ferris State homepage CTR from 1.6 percent to 14 percent using geo-targeting alone. Every technical and design decision in this proposal traces to one of those levers.

Comparable Drupal to WordPress migrations at premium higher education agencies typically run $250K to $450K or more. OHO Interactive prices in the $250K to $450K plus band, Kanopi Studios in the $200K to $400K band, Promet Source in the $175K to $350K band. Our base price delivers equivalent strategic depth, technical rigor, and accessibility compliance at a smaller number for two specific reasons: WordPress is inherently faster than Drupal for this scope (richer plugin ecosystem, shorter theme development cycle, lower CMS customization overhead), and our AI-assisted migration compresses the labor-intensive content work that inflates the other bids. The result is a defensible fixed fee that fits inside the institutional budget range and leaves room for the optional enrollment add-ons if and when the data justifies them.
