The four mistakes the current site makes

Every scene above traces back to one of four decisions we would undo on day one.

01
It optimizes for the wrong persona.

The current holyfamily.edu was built for the 18-year-old prospective who arrives in daylight, with a parent, clicking "Apply" as an abstract future verb. Maria is 27, alone, tired, and comparing four schools inside one browser session. Every pixel she lands on assumes she's on a campus tour.

The fix
A dedicated Adult Learner track in the IA — nights, mobile, price-first, program-first.
02
It hides the price.

The BSN program page never shows tuition. Not per credit, not per term, not total. The only path to a number is a form fill — a classic "gate the cost" pattern from 2014 enrollment marketing playbooks. Adult learners shopping four schools in one night will not fill out four forms. They will drop you from the list.

The fix
Price and time-to-degree above the fold on every program page. No exceptions.
03
The RFI form breaks on a phone.

The current Request Info form is a 12-field, three-column layout that was written for a desktop monitor and never reflowed for mobile. At 390px — the iPhone 14 viewport Maria is holding — fields overlap, the submit button hides behind the iOS keyboard, and validation errors scroll off-screen. It's not subtle. It's broken.

The fix
One smart form. Four fields on mobile. Pre-filled from URL parameters. Slate-wired.
04
There's no path for the impatient.

Every program page has exactly one conversion action — a "Request Information" button sitting five scrolls below the hero. Maria has 30 minutes, no patience, and no desire to start a conversation. She wants to browse, then apply. The current site has no lightweight browsing path, and no "I know what I want, take my money" path either.

The fix
Three above-the-fold CTAs per program: Get Info · See Cost · Apply Now.
"
If Maria leaves holyfamily.edu without an answer, no amount of brand marketing gets her back. Every redesign decision on this page — hero, pricing, form, schedule, CTA — is a decision we make with Maria's 30 minutes in mind.
