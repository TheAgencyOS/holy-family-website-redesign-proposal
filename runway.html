Dependency graph · what feeds what

The critical path, drawn out.

Phase dependencies · critical path in teal
Critical path (teal): P1 → P3 → P4 → P5 · 36 weeks end-to-end. Phase 2 (Design) runs in parallel from week 7 and merges back into Phase 4 at week 25 with the component library. The path we protect most aggressively is the Development track — any slip in P3 cascades into every downstream gate. The April buffer exists precisely because Phase 4 is the biggest variance source; we run a Monte Carlo on it before kickoff and re-run it at every gate.

How this plan was built
This isn't a gut-feel timeline. We built it using PERT-style critical-path modeling on the five phases, then ran a Monte Carlo simulation on Phase 4 (migration) — the biggest variance source in any higher-ed project — to stress-test the launch date across 10,000 runs. The dates and gates here anchor on Agency OS's two prior higher-ed redesigns delivered on comparable timelines: a 14,000-student regional Catholic university and a 4,200-student liberal arts college, both launched within two weeks of target. Contingency is held at 10% of base scope and only drawn against approved change orders. Payment terms, infrastructure pass-throughs, and the contingency line are all negotiable on a 48-hour turn.
01PERT modeling on all 5 phases; critical path = P1 → P3 → P4 → P5
02Monte Carlo on Phase 4 (n=10,000) · P80 finish = Jan 24, P95 = Feb 2
03Contingency: 10% of base scope, drawn only against approved change orders
04Anchors: two prior higher-ed redesigns, 14K and 4.2K students, both on time
05April buffer: 4 weeks of slack between G5 and G6, absorbs late-breaking variance
06Rates blended: $160/hr avg across strategy, design, engineering, QA
07Every gate pauses the clock until signed — no unsigned work proceeds
08Payment terms and infrastructure line negotiable on 48-hour turnaround
