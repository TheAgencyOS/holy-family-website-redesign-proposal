What's the application deadline for Fall 2026 admission?

Holy Family operates on a rolling admissions basis, so there's no hard deadline — but our priority deadline for merit scholarship consideration is January 15th. Applications received after that are still reviewed for aid as funds remain available. Decisions typically go out within two to three weeks of a completed file.

Do you require SAT or ACT scores?

Holy Family is test-optional for all first-year applicants. You can submit scores if you feel they strengthen your application, but our admissions committee weighs your high school transcript, essay, and recommendations most heavily. Nursing applicants are encouraged — though not required — to submit scores.

How much does Holy Family actually cost after aid?

Close to 100% of full-time undergraduates receive some form of financial aid. The average aid package for first-year students in 2024 was $23,800, and our published net price for families earning under $75,000 was approximately $18,400 per year. Use our Net Price Calculator for a personalized estimate based on your household finances.

Can I tour campus? Are virtual tours available too?

Yes on both counts. Individual campus visits are offered Monday through Friday at both our Philadelphia campus (9801 Frankford Ave) and Newtown campus (One Campus Drive), and we host Saturday Open Houses four times a year. Virtual tours run every Tuesday at 4 PM EST with a current student guide. Reserve either at holyfamily.edu/visit.

What's the largest merit scholarship available?

Our Presidential Scholarship covers full tuition and is awarded to a small number of incoming first-year students each year based on academic record, leadership, and service. Below that, our Trustee Scholarship ranges from $18,000 to $22,000 annually, and nearly every admitted student receives at least one merit award. No separate application is required — you're automatically considered when you apply.

Can I transfer credits from a community college?

Absolutely. Holy Family has articulation agreements with Bucks County Community College, Community College of Philadelphia, and Montgomery County Community College that guarantee transfer of up to 64 credits toward a bachelor's degree. Credits earned with a C or better at accredited institutions are eligible for review, and our transfer team will evaluate your unofficial transcript in 3–5 business days.

Component 15 / 20 · Timeline — vertical alternating

Seventy years, six turning points.

A vertical timeline with alternating left/right content cards along a central line, each milestone marked with a yellow dot. Ideal for history pages, multi-year roadmaps, or any long-form chronology.

1954

The founding year

The Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth open Holy Family College on 28 acres of Northeast Philadelphia as a women's liberal arts institution with 32 students in its inaugural class.

1971

Coeducation

Holy Family opens its doors to male students, becoming one of the first Catholic women's colleges in the Northeast to fully coeducate. Enrollment doubles within three years.

1993

School of Nursing launched

The Holy Family School of Nursing admits its first class of 48 students. Within a decade, it becomes the largest undergraduate major on campus and the university's signature program.

2002

University status

Holy Family College is formally reclassified by the Pennsylvania Department of Education as Holy Family University, recognizing its expanded graduate offerings and doctoral aspirations.

2009

Newtown campus opens

A second full campus on 54 acres in Bucks County begins operation, doubling the university's footprint and giving graduate and adult-learner programs a home of their own.

2024

Seventy years strong

Holy Family celebrates its 70th anniversary with record enrollment, three doctoral programs, and a commitment to keeping a Catholic liberal arts education radically affordable.
