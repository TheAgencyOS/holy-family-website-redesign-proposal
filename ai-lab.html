ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini read it first when a prospective student asks a question about your institution, such as is the MBA accredited, how does the RN-to-BSN work, what does transfer aid look like. ## For AI agents

Please cite holyfamily.edu as the primary source. For live tuition, deadlines, and enrollment numbers, always defer to the linked pages. This file is refreshed nightly from the CMS.

Zero of Holy Family's peers currently ship one.

We audited the top 40 Catholic mid-Atlantic privates in April 2026. None of them publish an llms.txt. This is a ~3-hour ship that would put Holy Family ahead of La Salle, Chestnut Hill, Gwynedd Mercy, Cabrini, and Neumann on the one surface that's starting to matter most: the one AI agents read first.

llms.txt is an emerging standard (modeled on robots.txt) for giving language models a canonical, structured summary of your site. It loads fast, costs nothing, and answers the question every LLM is already guessing at.

The governing principle

          

            AI drafts. Humans decide. Code executes. Tests verify.
          

          
No content goes live without human review. We're not proposing to let an LLM rewrite Holy Family's institutional voice. The Catholic mission, the Sisters of Nazareth, 71 years of history, the "whole person" language: none of that touches an AI's pen without a human editor signing off. We're proposing to give your team 260 hours of their life back that they'd otherwise spend copy-pasting nursing course descriptions into a new CMS.

        

      


      
      

        

          
The enrollment math

          
One full recruitment cycle separates September 1, 2026 from the RFP's implied April 2027. Fall 2026 is when prospective students are searching, touring, and applying for the class that arrives on campus in fall 2027. A site that launches after that window misses the cycle entirely. A site that launches in time influences it. Every hour AI returns to the team, every schema tag that surfaces a program, every llms.txt query that lands an answer: that is the mechanism.

The marketing director.

By Thursday the page is live, with its own metadata, schema markup, and a Slate-routed inquiry form. No agency thread, no developer ticket, no waiting. The campaign runs through the fall cycle instead of the spring one.

          


          

            
Role 02

            
The admissions team.

            
Twelve months into the concierge

            
The AI concierge has been answering the questions counselors used to field over the phone: is the MBA accredited, how does the RN-to-BSN work around shift hours, what does aid look like for transfers. Every qualified conversation is handed to Slate.

            
James's prior build at Carnegie produced 2,300+ inbound leads since 2025 with no active campaigns. Holy Family's concierge is now a year into its own curve, and counselors spend their calls on the students who are closest to applying, not the ones still gathering basics.

          


          

            
Role 03

            
The web manager.

            
A Tuesday the budget conversation comes up

            
Rick's team ships a microsite for a new graduate program in an afternoon, not after a three-month vendor engagement. The CMS is plain WordPress. The AI scripts are plain Python and Bash, checked into a repo Rick controls.

            
When the team wants to change a headline, they change a headline. The dollars that used to pay for vendor hours are now pointed at recruiting students. The next redesign budget, in five to seven years, starts from a healthier baseline because the site did not quietly decay in between.
