Seven openings · April 2026

There is a warmer Holy Family waiting to come forward online.

Ten days inside holyfamily.edu, five peer sites read side-by-side, and two hundred public reviews. What follows is seven openings we see — places where the real warmth of the campus is ready to step into the homepage, the adult-learner page, the program page. Each opening is also an enrollment number: a prospective student who would find the answer she needed, an inquiry that would become an application, an admit who would yield. Every quote is cited. Every estimate is labeled.

From the RFP itself

“Outdated design that doesn't reflect our vibrant campus culture. A dated user experience that doesn't meet prospective student expectations.”

Holy Family Website Redesign RFP · March 2026 · p. 3

The current site is a fine small-university website. The thing we keep returning to is more specific than “it could look newer.” It is that the warmth of the actual campus — the 91% who feel safe, the faculty who know every student by name, the monthly career fairs, the 83% first-year retention — is ready to show up in the first four seconds of a prospective student's visit. The seven openings below are the places where we think that warmth has the most room to come forward.

Seven openings

Ordered, roughly, by how much enrollment we think each one could add in the first year.

01

Your students have already written a warmer voice for you.

Read the homepage once, then read the first page of Niche reviews. Students describe Holy Family the way the RFP describes it — warm, small, attentive. The opportunity is to let that voice step onto the homepage: adopt the words students already use, and the site starts sounding like the campus.

“From the beginning, I felt a strong sense of community. The small class sizes made it easy to connect with professors, and the supportive environment encouraged me to stay focused on my goals.”

Current undergraduate · Niche.com · accessed April 2026

02

The adult-learner audience is ready for a page of its own.

The RFP names Adult and Graduate as the growth priority, and the opportunity here is direct: a dedicated flow for the 34-year-old nursing applicant researching her next step at 9:47 p.m. on her phone. Cost-per-credit, time-to-degree, and evening-class availability — the three things she needs above the fold — become the hero, and the traditional-age story keeps its own front door.

Student personas →
03

Accessibility is a reason to start fresh, not to retrofit.

The ADA Title II WCAG 2.1 AA compliance date for public-university digital content was April 24, 2026. A retrofit of 1,300 Drupal pages runs about 60–70% of a rebuild on its own. Building the new site with WCAG 2.2 AA in the design system from day one turns the timeline into an argument for, not against, a fresh build — and it lines up cleanly with the Fall 2026 recruitment cycle.

3-month runway →
04

The math is generous — a modest lift pays for the project.

On a funnel of roughly 60,000 monthly visitors (SEMrush est.) at a 1.5% inquiry rate and 22% inquiry-to-application, published redesign-plus-CRO benchmarks land between 25% and 35% lift in the first year. That is 50–70 incremental applications per month on the conservative end — enough to return the cost of the project long before year one closes.

Enrollment intelligence →
05

WordPress fits this team more naturally.

Drupal is built for a five-engineer team carrying a thousand-page library; the RFP names the preference for WordPress, and we agree. The opportunity is a CMS the Director of Digital Experience and one strategist can ship campaign pages from in days, not months. The migration path is well-trodden (FG Drupal → WP All Import, AI-assisted content QA, redirect parity), and the 1,300 pages compress to about 650 through the content audit you already have drafts of.

06

The public reviews are already written. The website is the reply.

Prospective students read Niche, Glassdoor, and Rate My Professors before they open holyfamily.edu. The site is the only place where the school gets to tell its own longer story — with named students, quoted alumni, faculty letters, and outcome data by program. Right now that longer story isn't there, and the critical reviews are the only voice in the conversation.

07

Prospective students now start with a conversation.

Half of prospective students already use AI tools weekly. AI referrals convert at roughly 4.4× organic. holyfamily.edu currently has no llms.txt, no EducationalOccupationalProgram JSON-LD on program pages, and no FAQ-pattern schema. The fix is roughly one engineer, one week, and the return is disproportionate to the effort.
