Holy Family · AI for enrollment

Seven ideas that help Holy Family enroll.

Each idea below earns its place by answering one question — does this help Holy Family enroll more of the right students? None are experimental. Each has a working analogue in production today.

Chapter I · Included What ships with the site. Three · in the base Chapter II · Optional What Holy Family can add. Three · priced separately Chapter III · Ongoing What we hand back. One · quarterly
Chapter I · Included · three ideas

What ships with the site.

Three enrollment-shaped features live inside the $145,000 base. No further decision required; no line-item surprises at launch.

01

When a family asks AI about Holy Family, Holy Family answers.

The site ships with an llms.txt file, schema markup across every program page, and canonical page structure — the pieces that decide whether an AI assistant cites holyfamily.edu directly, or a third-party ranking site instead.

Included · in base scope AI discoverability
a
Answer, cited
ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini link back to HFU pages by name, not to a third-party list.
s
Program schema at launch
Every program, tuition line, and deadline exposed as schema.org that assistants can read.
m
Measured before and after
We baseline citation share in July 2026 and re-check at 30 and 90 days post-launch.
02

Campaign pages, in days, not months.

A WordPress workflow that lets marketing draft, structure, and ship a new program landing page from a short brief — audience, outcomes, differentiators. Every draft human-edited before it goes live. Templates and the prompt library belong to HFU on day one.

Included · in base scope Marketing speed
b
Brief in, draft out
Audience, outcomes, three differentiators, one call to action. The AI fills the scaffold; the team edits the copy.
h
Human-edited, always
No draft reaches the public site without a human pass. Voice stays Holy Family's; speed comes from removing the blank page.
o
The team owns it
Templates, prompt library, and approval workflow transfer to marketing at go-live. No dependency on our team to ship page ten.
03

A Monday memo, not a dashboard.

Three things that moved the funnel last week. Two pages that are leaking. One experiment worth running. GA4 and Slate translated into short recommendations — not a screenshot. The event taxonomy and the memo template stay with the internal team.

Included · in base scope Weekly intelligence
3
Three up, two down
What moved, what's leaking — with the specific page and the specific number, not a colored arrow.
1
One experiment to run
A single recommended A/B or copy change for the week ahead, sized to a marketing team of two.
p
Plain English
GA4 + Slate translated into sentences. Dashboards are still there for anyone who wants them.
✦
Chapter II · Optional · three ideas

What Holy Family can add.

Optional add-ons answered against the RFP's Section E. Activate at launch — or months later, when the data says it's time.

04

A 24/7 admissions counselor, on the site.

A conversational assistant that answers prospective-student questions in the voice of admissions, captures inquiries into Slate, and hands off to a counselor when the question warrants it. James Vineburgh's prior build at a peer Catholic institution is the reference implementation.

Optional · priced separately Admissions concierge
✦
2,300+ leads, no campaign spend
From James's prior build since early 2025 — inbound only, one institution, conversational.
s
Slate-captured
Inquiries land in the CRM as structured leads, not a transcript silo. Counselors see the context.
h
Human hand-off
The assistant knows when to stop answering and book a human — usually at the first financial-aid question.
05

Search that understands what nursing fits a full-time job.

Direct answers instead of a list of keyword matches. Internal queries surface back to the marketing team as a standing source of content briefs — what prospects are actually looking for, in their own words.

Optional · priced separately Conversational search
a
Intent, not keywords
"What programs fit around a full-time job" returns a ranked answer, not a list.
b
A standing content brief
Marketing sees the searches no one has answered yet — the page ideas write themselves.
v
Tuned to admissions voice
Not a generic model output. The answer reads like the rest of the site.
06

For the student at 9:47 pm, after the kids are asleep.

A narrower concierge built for adult and graduate prospects — the RFP's Goal #2 audience. It walks through transfer evaluation, a realistic timeline, costs after aid, and the specific next step. No sales tone. Doesn't ask for an email three times.

Optional · priced separately Adult-learner pathway
t
Transfer credit, honestly
A realistic read on what transfers in, not a marketing estimate.
$
Cost after aid, not before
The number a working parent actually needs, with the caveats named.
→
One next step
Not three email-capture walls. One specific action, with a named person on the other side.
✦
Chapter III · Ongoing · one idea

What we hand back.

A light quarterly retainer. Small enough to be worth it; short enough to hand back to the internal team after the first two cycles.

07

Four times a year, we check the machines.

We audit what ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini currently say about Holy Family — tuition, deadlines, program names, accreditation — and correct any drift at the source. A two-page report. After two cycles, the internal team runs it.

Ongoing · quarterly AI response monitor
4
Four assistants, quarterly
ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini. The same ten questions a family would ask.
d
Drift corrected at the source
Tuition, deadlines, program names, accreditation — fixed in the CMS, re-indexed, verified.
↵
Handed back
After two cycles, the playbook and the prompt set stay with Holy Family. The internal team runs it from there.
A note on how we chose these

Every idea on this page has a working analogue in production today, most on work our team has shipped. If an idea didn't trace back to enrollment, we cut it before it reached the page.
