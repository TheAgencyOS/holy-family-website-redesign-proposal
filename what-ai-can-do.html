I  ·  Included with the base
Idea 01  /  AI discoverability & SEO

Holy Family's site answers when a family asks AI about Holy Family.

The site ships with a canonical llms.txt file, schema.org markup on every program page (CollegeOrUniversity, EducationalOccupationalProgram, Course, Offer, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList), a tuned robots.txt that welcomes GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended, and a citation baseline measured in July 2026 with 30- and 90-day re-checks. We verify Holy Family's Google Knowledge Panel and submit structured answers for the questions prospective families actually ask.

  • llms.txtCanonical program list, tuition, deadlines, and accreditation. One file every assistant reads.
  • SchemaSix schema.org types wired per page. Programs exposed as machine-readable offers.
  • Crawler policyrobots.txt tuned for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended.
  • BaselineTen standard questions across four assistants, July 2026. Re-checked at 30 and 90 days.
  • Knowledge panelVerified Google claim, People Also Ask answers structured and submitted.
  • Citation shareTracked quarterly: when an assistant answers about HFU, does it link back or not?
How it
helps enroll

When a family asks ChatGPT "is Holy Family a good nursing school?", the answer cites holyfamily.edu directly, not a third-party ranking site whose copy you don't control. More citations become more direct traffic, more RFIs, and more applications through a channel most universities have left entirely to chance.

Idea 02  /  AI page generation

From brief to shipped page, in a day.

A WordPress Gutenberg workflow where marketing fills a one-screen brief (audience, three outcomes, three differentiators, proof points, primary CTA) and the system returns a full draft landing page: hero, outcome sections, FAQ block, SEO title and meta, EducationalOccupationalProgram schema, alt text, and internal links back to financial aid and related programs. Every draft lands in a side-by-side diff review and never goes public without a human click. Tuition, deadlines, and aid figures are pulled from canonical CMS fields, so the system cannot hallucinate a number.

How it
helps enroll

Marketing ships 5× more landing pages without waiting on an agency. Every new audience (transfer nurses, working parents, Philadelphia-area adults, international students) gets its own tailored page in days, not months, and every page is a new doorway into the funnel.

Included · base scope Gutenberg blocks 12+ templates
  • Brief One-screen form: audience, three outcomes, three differentiators, proof points, primary CTA. Replaces the blank page.
  • Draft Gutenberg blocks, not a text dump. Hero, outcomes, FAQ, and CTA all arrive as real editable blocks on HFU's brand.
  • Facts Tuition, deadlines, accreditation pulled from CMS. Single source of truth. Impossible to hallucinate a number.
  • SEO Title, meta, schema, alt text, internal links generated per page. Indexable and AEO-ready on day one.
  • Review Side-by-side diff. Approve, edit in Gutenberg, or reject. Nothing publishes without a human click.
  • Library 12+ templates for common campaigns: transfer, graduate, adult, accelerated, scholarship, international, corporate.
Idea 03  /  Autoblogging & content engine

A content engine that proposes, never publishes.

A weekly pipeline that mines the questions prospective students are asking (through site search, the admissions chatbot, internal search logs, and Google Search Console) and returns three blog-post drafts every Monday morning in Holy Family's voice, each with SEO title, meta, Article schema, internal links, and alt text. They land in the marketing inbox as Gutenberg drafts. Nothing publishes without a human click. Once approved, posts are scheduled, image-tagged from the HFU media library, and tracked in Google Search Console so next week's topic brief learns from what worked.

How it
helps enroll

Holy Family publishes 12 approved posts a month, or 144 pieces of indexed content a year, each targeting a real question a prospective student already asked. Every post is a search-engine doorway into the funnel that the site didn't have the day before.

Idea 04  ·  24/7 Admissions Chatbot

A counselor who's awake when the family is.

A conversational assistant built on retrieval-augmented generation over 400+ Holy Family pages: program descriptions, syllabi, financial-aid FAQs, application deadlines, and student-life content. It answers in the voice of admissions (few-shot tuned to real counselor replies), captures each conversation as a structured Slate contact record with name, email, program interest, and transcript, and books a human counselor the moment a question crosses the hand-off rules, typically the first specific financial-aid or graduate-application question.

  • RAG corpus400+ pages embedded and re-indexed on every publish: programs, aid, academics, student life.
  • VoiceFew-shot tuned to real admissions responses. Reads like a counselor, not a generic model.
  • Slate-capturedEvery conversation lands as a structured lead (name, email, program, stage, transcript), not a silo.
  • Human hand-offFinancial aid, graduate apps, and international: rules trigger a booking with a named counselor.
  • MultilingualEnglish, Spanish, and Portuguese, matched to Philadelphia's prospective-student demographics.
  • Always onAnswers at 9 pm, 11 pm, 2 am: the hours an admissions office isn't staffed and prospects are researching.
How it
helps enroll

The reference build has generated more than 2,300 inbound leads since early 2025 at a peer Catholic institution, with no campaign spend. Pure organic capture at hours no admissions office is open. Every conversation becomes a Slate lead a counselor can follow up on Monday morning.

James Vineburgh's prior build at a peer Catholic institution. Same architecture, re-tuned to Holy Family's voice and Slate instance.

Idea 05  /  Conversational site search

Search that understands intent.

A site search that embeds every program page, blog post, and the full financial-aid library into a vector index, classifies query intent (info / price / apply / compare), and returns a direct answer with cited sources, not a list of keyword matches. Every query is logged. The top 50 questions each week become marketing's standing content brief for the autoblogging engine.

  • Vector index1,200+ pages embedded; re-indexed on every publish.
  • Intent classifierRoutes query to info, price, apply, or compare. Each has its own response shape.
  • Cited answersEvery response links to the source page. No unsourced claims.
  • Weekly briefTop 50 queries delivered to marketing Monday morning. Feeds the content engine.
How it
helps enroll

"What nursing program fits around a full-time job" returns a direct answer instead of twelve keyword matches. Prospects find the right page on the first try, and marketing sees every unanswered question, so the pages write themselves because prospects asked for them in their own words.

Optional · priced separately Vector / RAG Intent > keywords
Idea 06  ·  Adult-learner pathway

For the student at 9:47 pm, after the kids are asleep.

A narrower concierge, built for the RFP's Goal #2 audience: adult and graduate prospects. It walks through transfer evaluation using registrar-approved credit tables (not a marketing estimate), models cost after aid against actual Pell and Pennsylvania state-grant eligibility, knows the FAFSA calendar, and offers a single next step, with a named counselor on the other side.

The number a working parent actually needs is cost after aid, not before, with the caveats named. The answer on transfer credit is what the registrar will actually approve, not a recruiter's guess. And the close isn't three email-capture walls. It's one specific action: book a 20-minute call with Maria on Tuesday at 8:15 pm.

The page meets a prospective student at the only hour of the day that's actually theirs. It respects that hour by answering in one screen, not walking them through a funnel.

How it
helps enroll

RFP Goal #2 is adult learners, and adult learners research at night. A generic site treats them like traditional freshmen. This pathway answers the three questions they actually have (transfer credit, cost after aid, one next step) and books the follow-up inside the one window the day gives them.

III  ·  Ongoing, quarterly
Idea 07  /  AI response monitor

Four times a year, we check the machines.

Every quarter we ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini the same ten questions a prospective family would ask: tuition, deadlines, program names, accreditation, aid, transfer credit, accelerated paths, graduate options, locations, and contact info. We flag what's wrong, correct the drift at the CMS source (not through prompt engineering), re-generate the llms.txt and sitemap, re-submit to Google and Bing, and verify the fix in the next cycle. Two pages, not a dashboard.

  • Scope10 prompts × 4 assistants = 40 audited answers per quarter.
  • Fix at sourceDrift corrected in the CMS, not hidden in a prompt. Re-indexed, re-submitted, verified.
  • ScorecardCitation rate, accuracy, and recency, tracked quarter over quarter.
  • HandbackAt Q3, the playbook, prompts, and scoring rubric stay with Holy Family. Internal team runs Q4.
How it
helps enroll

LLMs drift. A program name changes, an aid figure updates, an accreditation is renewed, and the assistants keep answering with stale data for months. The audit catches it, fixes it at the source, and verifies the correction, so every citation keeps pointing prospective families at the right answer.
