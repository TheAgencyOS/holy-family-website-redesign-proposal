Deadlines, by audience

Dates on one page.

Fall and spring admission cycles, with the priority windows that most affect your aid package. The item marked “current” is the next date relevant to most applicants.

  1. Feb 1 · 2026
    Spring transfer priority

    Priority deadline for transfer students beginning in the spring term. Students who meet it get the best access to transfer scholarships and preferred course registration.

  2. Mar 1 · 2026
    FAFSA priority for first-year

    Submit the FAFSA by March 1 to receive your institutional aid package alongside your admissions decision.

  3. Apr 26 · 2026
    Spring Open House

    Campus visit weekend for high school juniors and seniors and transfer students. Academic workshops, financial aid walkthrough, and student-ambassador-led tours.

  4. May 1 · 2026
    Enrollment deposit due

    National candidate reply date for admitted first-year students. $150 deposit, refundable through this date.

  5. Jun 15 · 2026
    Graduate priority · Fall

    Priority deadline for most graduate programs starting in the fall. Some clinical programs (nursing, counseling) close earlier. Check program pages.

  6. Aug 15 · 2026
    Final first-year deadline

    Last day to apply and enroll for the fall undergraduate term. Rolling review, but space in some programs is limited by August.

  7. Oct 15 · 2026
    Spring 2027 priority for all

    Priority window for first-year, transfer, graduate, and adult-learner applications beginning the spring 2027 term.

  8. Dec 1 · 2026
    Spring enrollment deposit

    Enrollment deposit due for students admitted for the spring 2027 term. Rolling admissions continue until term start.

If a date is close or just passed

Holy Family is a rolling-admissions university. If a priority deadline has slipped, we still read applications and we still build aid packages. The earlier windows just give you the widest set of options. Call your counselor before deciding a date has passed.

Admissions FAQ

The six questions we hear most.

If your question isn't here, your counselor has heard it before. Book a 15-minute call and ask.

Do you require test scores?
Holy Family is test-optional for first-year applicants. If you have strong SAT or ACT scores and believe they help your story, you are welcome to send them — they can unlock additional merit consideration. Not submitting scores does not disadvantage your application.
How do transfer credits work?
Use our Transfer Credit Calculator to preview what credits apply before you submit an application. Once admitted, our registrar performs a formal evaluation of every course on your transcript. Most transfer students bring thirty or more credits with them.
What is rolling admission?
Rolling admission means we read and decide on applications as they come in, rather than waiting for a single deadline. That usually means a decision within two to three weeks of a complete file. Priority dates still matter — they protect your access to institutional scholarships and preferred course registration.
Is there an application fee?
No. The undergraduate application is free. Most graduate programs also waive the fee for applicants who begin their application through an admissions counselor or an Instant Decision Day.
How is aid actually calculated?
Once we receive your FAFSA we know what federal and state aid you are eligible for. Your counselor then builds an institutional package — merit scholarships from your application, need-based grants from your FAFSA, and work-study where eligible. You receive one packaged award letter with a plain-language breakdown of cost after aid.
What is the adult-learner track?
Our adult-learner track is designed for students returning to finish a degree or starting one after time in the workforce. Evening, weekend, and fully-online cohorts; credit for prior learning of up to thirty credits where eligible; and an advising team experienced with employer-benefit billing and degree-completion planning.
