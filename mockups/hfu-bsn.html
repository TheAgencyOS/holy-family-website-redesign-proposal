The arc at a glance

Your four years, briefly.

Foundations, then clinical basics, then specialty rotations, then a capstone semester that prepares you for the NCLEX.

  1. Year 1 · Foundations
    Sciences, liberal arts core, and the nursing lens

    Anatomy, physiology, microbiology, developmental psychology, theology of caring. You are formed as a whole person before you are formed as a clinician.

  2. Year 2 · Clinical basics
    Assessment, pharmacology, and your first clinical rotation

    Health assessment, pharmacology, fundamentals of nursing practice. First clinical placement at a partner hospital, typically medical-surgical.

    Typical fall cohort
  3. Year 3 · Specialty rotations
    Adult health, maternal/child, mental health, community

    Rotations across adult medical-surgical, obstetrics, pediatrics, psychiatric-mental health, and community/public health. Evidence-based practice and research methods run alongside.

  4. Year 4 · Capstone + NCLEX prep
    Leadership, preceptorship, and licensure readiness

    Nursing leadership and management, a final preceptored clinical with a practicing RN, and a structured NCLEX-RN preparation track through the spring semester.

Specialty pathways

Where our graduates practice.

Four representative concentrations our BSN graduates move into after licensure. Students can tailor electives and capstone placements toward any of them.

Acute care covers the fast-paced, high-acuity settings where registered nurses manage complex medical-surgical patients, intensive care, and emergency populations. The pathway emphasizes clinical reasoning under pressure, interdisciplinary teamwork, and the technology-rich environments of teaching hospitals.

Holy Family acute-care graduates typically begin on medical-surgical floors at partner health systems and step up to telemetry, step-down, ICU, or ED within one to three years of practice.

  • Medical-surgical units
  • Intensive care
  • Emergency department
  • Telemetry & step-down
  • Post-anesthesia care
  • Cardiac catheterization
School of Nursing · Elective Acute

Acute-care capstone track

Senior year Preceptored

A guided capstone placement inside a regional teaching hospital, paired with acute-care-focused electives in pharmacology, critical-care foundations, and rapid-response teaming.

360 clinical hrs NUR 410 / 430

Community health extends nursing practice outside the hospital: home-health, public-health departments, school nursing, hospice, and community-clinic care. The pathway is particularly meaningful for students drawn to population health, health-equity work, and care of under-resourced populations.

Community placements often build on Holy Family's long-standing relationships with Philadelphia-area public-health agencies and community health centers.

  • Home health agencies
  • Public health departments
  • School nursing
  • Hospice & palliative care
  • Community health clinics
  • Occupational health
School of Nursing · Elective Community

Community & public-health track

Year 3 - 4 Field placement

An extended field rotation with a Philadelphia-area public-health department or community clinic, layered on the standard community/public-health nursing course in the third year.

180 field hrs NUR 340 +

Pediatric nursing focuses on the care of infants, children, and adolescents across the continuum. Placements range from well-child clinics to pediatric intensive-care units. The pathway pairs developmental psychology with family-centered care and age-specific clinical skills.

Placements include a regional children's hospital and several pediatric specialty clinics across the network.

  • Pediatric medical-surgical
  • Neonatal intensive care
  • Pediatric ICU
  • Pediatric emergency
  • Children's specialty clinics
  • Pediatric oncology
School of Nursing · Elective Peds

Pediatric focus track

Senior year Specialty

Pediatric capstone placements paired with electives in pediatric pharmacology, growth and development, and family-centered care across the illness continuum.

240 clinical hrs NUR 325 / 430

Gerontology prepares nurses to care for older adults across acute, long-term, and community settings. The pathway emphasizes chronic-disease management, cognitive health, palliative approaches, and the social determinants that shape aging well.

Placements span regional long-term care facilities, geriatric clinics, and memory-care units.

  • Long-term care facilities
  • Geriatric outpatient clinics
  • Memory-care units
  • Hospice & palliative care
  • Home-based primary care
  • Rehabilitation & sub-acute
School of Nursing · Elective Gero

Gerontology focus track

Year 3 - 4 Emerging field

Capstone placements in long-term care and memory-care settings, paired with electives in gerontological pharmacology, palliative approaches, and chronic-disease management.

200 clinical hrs NUR 430 +

The credentials

What makes this degree portable.

Accreditation is the reason your BSN still counts when you move, apply to graduate school, or sit for a license in another state.

  1. CCNE
    Accredited · since 2008

    Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education

    The national standard for baccalaureate and graduate nursing programs. The credential your future employer, state board, and graduate program will look for.

    Verify on the CCNE registry
  2. PA
    SBON
    Approved

    Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing

    State-level program approval. Graduates are eligible for Pennsylvania RN licensure and for the reciprocal licensure pathways in every other state.

    Verify on the PA SBON registry
  3. NCLEX
    Eligible to sit · all 50 states

    NCLEX-RN Licensure Examination

    Graduates qualify to sit for the National Council Licensure Examination for Registered Nurses. It is the single licensure exam recognized across U.S. jurisdictions.

    Learn about the NCLEX-RN
  4. MSCHE
    Accredited · institution-wide

    Middle States Commission on Higher Education

    The regional accreditation Holy Family holds as an institution. It is the credential that makes every course you take here transferable and every dollar of federal aid eligible.

    Verify on the MSCHE registry

Full disclosure Program accreditation status and dates are reviewed with the School of Nursing during discovery. External registry links above are the authoritative record.
