About the school

A calling older than the university itself.

Long before Holy Family opened its doors in 1954, the Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth were already at the bedsides of Philadelphia's poor and forgotten. When the University was founded, nursing was named at its center — not as a department, but as the living expression of the Sisters' charism of healing ministry.

Seventy years later, the School of Nursing & Health Sciences carries that same mission: to form clinicians whose competence is matched by conscience, whose practice is grounded in evidence, and whose care is rooted in the dignity of every human person. Every course, every simulation, every clinical rotation is shaped by that purpose.

Our undergraduate and graduate programs are fully accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE) and recognized by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing. Graduates practice across the region's most trusted hospital systems — and increasingly, lead them.

Small seminars, real clinical judgment.

With a 13:1 student-to-faculty ratio, your instructors know your name by the second week. Every senior clinical course is capped at 10 students per rotation, ensuring every BSN graduate has been individually coached at the bedside by a practicing RN.

  • 13:1 student-to-faculty ratio across all nursing programs
  • Senior clinicals capped at 10 students per rotation
  • Dedicated NCLEX readiness faculty & ATI integration

Faculty who still practice at the bedside.

Our instructors are not academics who used to nurse — they are nurses who also teach. Full-time faculty maintain active clinical appointments at Jefferson, Penn, Temple, and Einstein, bringing real cases into the classroom every week.

  • Active clinical appointments at Jefferson, Penn & Temple
  • FNP faculty maintain independent primary-care panels
  • Case-based teaching drawn from the prior week's shifts

Research that changes practice, not just publishes.

Our faculty lead funded studies on fall prevention in long-term care, parish-based chronic disease screening, and simulation pedagogy. Undergraduate BSN students have presented alongside faculty at the Sigma Theta Tau International Biennial Convention.

  • Sigma Theta Tau Mu Omega chapter on campus
  • Undergraduate research fellowships & summer stipends
  • DNP scholarly projects deployed at partner hospitals

Rooted in Philadelphia's own neighborhoods.

From free blood-pressure clinics in Northeast Philadelphia parishes to the mobile screening unit that visits senior centers, our faculty lead a year-round public health presence. Community health isn't a module — it's a thread through every BSN year.

  • Mobile health unit running 40+ community events per year
  • Parish-based chronic disease screening partnerships
  • Global health immersion trips to Nicaragua & Uganda
Inside our school

Where students become nurses.

The physical and human infrastructure of nursing training at Holy Family spans two campuses, the ETC Tower, thirty hospital systems, and a mobile clinic that's on the road four days a week.

01 · On Campus

Clinical Simulation Center

Two full ICU bays, an OB suite with high-fidelity maternal-neonate simulators, pediatric Laerdal mannequins, and observation-debrief rooms with one-way glass. Students run full code scenarios before they ever step onto a real unit.

Tour the simulation center
02 · On Campus

Health Assessment Lab

Six fully equipped head-to-toe physical assessment bays, complete with hospital-grade exam tables, otoscope/ophthalmoscope stations, and trained standardized patients who give students real-time feedback on bedside manner and communication.

See the lab equipment
03 · Across Philadelphia

30+ Partner Hospital Network

Clinical rotations at Jefferson Health, Penn Medicine, Temple Health, Einstein Medical Center, St. Christopher's Hospital for Children, Aria-Jefferson Torresdale, Holy Redeemer, and many more — a geographic footprint that mirrors where our graduates ultimately practice.

Browse partner hospitals
04 · In the Community

Community Health Outreach

A mobile screening clinic runs four days per week, visiting parish partners, senior centers, and underserved neighborhoods across Northeast Philadelphia and lower Bucks County. BSN seniors staff it as a capstone clinical — learning what "patient" means outside the hospital walls.

See the outreach schedule
