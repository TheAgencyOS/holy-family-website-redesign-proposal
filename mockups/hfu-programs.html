Section 04 · How to choose

How to choose a program.

Four questions our admissions advisors walk prospective students through. The answers often shape everything else.

What do I want to do after I graduate?

Start with the profession or the field before the program. Advisors at Holy Family begin every meeting by asking what you are drawn to rather than what's on a menu. Nursing, education, psychology, accounting, and computer science are all paths into specific licensures and roles; arts and sciences majors like English, history, and philosophy sit at the foundation of law, policy, journalism, and ministry.

If you are not sure yet, a liberal-arts major paired with an internship sequence and faculty mentorship is often the right first move. You will not lose time.

What fits the life I have right now?

We build schedules around working life, not around a traditional calendar. Online RN-to-BSN, evening MBA cohorts, hybrid doctoral residencies, and weekend certificate sequences are all designed for students who are already in a profession. Most graduate programs can be completed at the pace that works for you, from accelerated to two or three years.

Our Newtown campus serves Bucks County commuters; our Philadelphia campus is minutes from I-95 and the Market-Frankford line.

Can I transfer credits I already have?

Yes. Holy Family has transfer articulation agreements with regional community colleges and accepts credit for prior learning from accredited institutions. A transfer advisor will review your transcript and provide a written credit evaluation before you enroll, so there are no surprises about your time to graduation.

Adults with prior professional experience may also be eligible for credit through the Council for Adult and Experiential Learning (CAEL).

Are there stackable credentials I can earn along the way?

Several of our certificates stack directly into degree programs. The Cybersecurity Certificate, for example, counts as four courses toward the B.A. in Cybersecurity and IT Management. The Pastoral Ministry Certificate can roll into a Religious Studies minor. Teacher-endorsement certificates add to an active PA teaching license without requiring a second degree.

This lets you earn a credential now, put it to work, and return to finish the degree on your own timeline.

Not sure where to start?

An admissions counselor will walk you through the program that fits. One conversation, no pressure, and a written credit evaluation if you are transferring.
