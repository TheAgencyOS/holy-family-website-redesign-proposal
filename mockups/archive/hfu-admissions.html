Is Holy Family test-optional?

Yes. SAT and ACT scores are optional for first-year applicants. If you feel your scores represent you well, we welcome them — but our admissions team evaluates every application holistically based on your transcripts, essay, and story.

What’s the average aid package?

Close to 100% of our full-time undergraduates receive some form of financial aid, and the average first-year aid package combines institutional merit scholarships, need-based grants, and federal aid. Use our Net Price Calculator to see your personalized estimate in about five minutes.

How quickly will I get an admissions decision?

Holy Family operates on rolling admissions, so we review applications as they’re submitted. Most first-year applicants receive a decision within two to three weeks of their file being complete. Transfer students attending our Instant Decision Day receive a decision the same day.

Can I apply for free?

Yes. There is no application fee for first-year, transfer, or graduate applicants. You can apply directly through our portal or via the Common Application.

What if I’m an international student?

We welcome students from around the world. Our international admissions team will guide you through TOEFL/IELTS requirements, credential evaluations, and the I-20 process. You’ll have a dedicated counselor from the moment you inquire.

Do I have to be Catholic to attend?

No. Holy Family is a Catholic university rooted in the charism of the Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth, but we welcome students of all faiths and backgrounds. Our Catholic identity shapes our community’s values of compassion, hospitality, and service — values that touch every student regardless of faith tradition.
