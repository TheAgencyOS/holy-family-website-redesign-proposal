A Letter from the President
Dear future student,

You are reading this because something — a photograph, a sentence, a recommendation — made you curious about Holy Family. I hope what I write here answers a little of that curiosity.

We are a small Catholic university in Northeast Philadelphia, with a quieter campus in Newtown, and we have been teaching students to assume lifelong responsibilities since 1954. Our tradition comes from the Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth, who believed that education is an act of loving attention — and we still run the place as if that is true.

If you visit, you will find small classes, faculty who know your name by the end of the first week, and an affordability you may not expect from a private university. If you stay, you will find yourself part of something that was built over seventy years to outlast whatever is making you anxious right now.

Come walk the quad. Bring your family. Ask the hardest questions you have.

— Anne M. Prisco, Ph.D.
Sixth President, Holy Family University
