Why Holy Family

Four things families tell us actually matter.

Four honest answers to the question admitted students ask on every campus walk. Written the way an admissions counselor would actually say them.

01 Faith and intellect, held together
The Nazareth tradition reads the Catholic intellectual life as a serious academic discipline, not a set of side requirements. Students of every faith are welcome. The invitation is to take questions about meaning, service, and human dignity as seriously as any other coursework they’ll do here.
02 Class sizes that mean you’re known
A 13:1 student-to-faculty ratio and an average class size under twenty. That means your professor knows when you’re struggling before you send the email. Your advisor knows your name the first time you walk into the office, not the fifteenth. Illustrative: a freshman composition section at Holy Family usually has 16 people in it.
03 Philadelphia as classroom
Clinical rotations at Temple Health, Jefferson, and regional hospital systems. Teacher placements across the School District of Philadelphia. Internships in finance, cyber, and biotech within a SEPTA ride of campus. The city is part of the curriculum, not an afterthought tacked onto senior year.
04 Outcomes that match the investment
Ranked a top-three best-value college in the Philadelphia area and the Safest College in Philadelphia by Niche, back-to-back. Approximately 92% of graduates are working or enrolled in graduate school within a year of commencement (Career Development Services). When families weigh Holy Family against a state school, the math is usually closer than they expect.
