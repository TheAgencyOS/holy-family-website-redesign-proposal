What a visit feels like

A typical 90-minute tour.

No pressure and no script. A current student walks you through it. You can stop, skip steps, or stay longer at what you care about.

  1. Min 0 · Arrival

    Check in at the admissions welcome desk

    Name tag, a campus map, and a quick hello from the counselor assigned to your region. Coffee, tea, and water on the sideboard.

  2. Min 10 · Orientation

    Meet your student ambassador

    A current HFU student introduces themselves and asks what you'd like to see first. They are often in your program of interest.

  3. Min 20 · The walk

    Walk the campus the way students live it

    The quad, residence halls (Philadelphia) or adult-learner commons (Newtown), a classroom, and at least one program-specific space: nursing sim lab, teaching studio, or advising center.

  4. Min 50 · Program deep-dive

    Sit in on a class or meet a faculty member

    When classes are in session, your ambassador can drop you into a lecture in your area of interest. If not, we arrange a brief chat with a faculty member or program director.

  5. Min 70 · Admissions & aid

    One-on-one with your counselor

    Private conversation about your application, aid questions, transfer credit, and the steps that make sense from where you are right now.

  6. Min 90 · Wrap

    Next steps. Or none at all.

    If you're ready, we talk application timeline. If you aren't, that's fine too. You leave with a packet you can re-read later and an email address for follow-up questions.

Before you come

Visit FAQ.

The questions we're asked most. If yours isn't here, email admissions@holyfamily.edu. A counselor will reply within one business day.

What should I bring?
Just yourself and a list of questions. Transfer students attending Instant Decision Day should also bring an official transcript. We can do a credit evaluation on the spot. Everything else is handled at check-in.
Can my family come with me?
Yes, and we encourage it. Most visits include a parent, guardian, spouse, or partner. Please add them when you book so your ambassador knows the group size, and let us know if anyone needs accessibility accommodations.
Is there parking on either campus?
Free visitor parking at both campuses. Philadelphia visitors use the main lot off Frankford Avenue; Newtown visitors use the lot at One Campus Drive. A parking pass is emailed with your booking confirmation.
Do you offer weekend or evening options?
Yes. Saturday tours run twice a month at 10:00 am and 1:00 pm, and we offer evening tours at 6:00 pm during the academic year. Adult-learner info sessions are Tuesday and Thursday evenings at the Newtown campus.
Can I take a virtual tour instead?
Yes. The virtual tour is a self-paced 360 walk-through of both campuses with narration from current students. It's not a substitute for being here in person, but it's a useful first look, especially for out-of-state families.
