Your day, minute by minute

What to expect on the day.

Every visit day looks a little different depending on what you came for, but here’s the shape of our most common itinerary — the one families tell us feels just right.

  1. 8:30am
    Stop 01

    Arrive & check in

    Pull up to the Admissions House at the base of ETC Tower. Visitor parking is marked and free, and you’ll find a student greeter at the door with your name badge and day’s schedule.

    Coffee & pastries on us
  2. 9:00am
    Stop 02

    Welcome from a dean

    Meet one of the deans of our four schools of study — Nursing & Health Sciences, Business, Education, or Arts & Sciences — for a 20-minute welcome and Q&A on what makes each school tick.

  3. 9:30am
    Stop 03

    Student-led campus tour

    A 90-minute walk through the quad, chapel, residence halls, science labs, and library — led by a student ambassador who’ll answer every question you bring. This is the heart of the day.

    90 minutes outdoors & in
  4. 11:00am
    Stop 04

    Financial aid, in plain English

    Our financial aid office walks families through exactly how aid packaging works at Holy Family — the FAFSA, merit scholarships, grants, loans, work-study, and the Net Price Calculator. Bring questions; we’ll bring coffee.

  5. 12:00pm
    Stop 05

    Lunch in the dining hall

    You and your family eat lunch with current students in the main dining hall — included. It’s the most-reported best part of every family’s visit, and it’s where the campus really shows itself.

    Meal included for up to 4 guests
  6. 1:00pm
    Stop 06

    1:1 with your counselor

    Every visitor meets one-on-one with their admissions counselor. This is your chance to ask anything — about majors, transfer credits, scholarships, housing, or what happens after you apply. No two meetings are the same.

  7. 2:00pm
    Stop 07

    Q&A and send-off

    We end where we started. Families gather for a last round of questions, free next-steps materials, and a parting gift. Most families linger — and that’s exactly what we hope for.

Getting here, staying, eating.

Traveling from out of town? Here’s the short list of what locals recommend for the practical parts of a campus visit.

Getting here

Both campuses are easy to reach by car, and Philadelphia is well-served by SEPTA. Visitor parking is free and marked.

  • By carI-95 Exit 32 (Academy Rd) or PA Turnpike Exit 351 · 20 min from Center City
  • By SEPTARoute 14 bus stops directly at the Philadelphia campus entrance
  • By airportPHL airport is 35 minutes by car or 50 minutes by regional rail
