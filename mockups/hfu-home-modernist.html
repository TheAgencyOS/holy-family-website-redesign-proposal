About · Mission

Educating students to assume lifelong responsibilities.

Holy Family University is a Catholic, coeducational institution sponsored by the Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth. Since 1954 we have been teaching students to assume lifelong responsibilities — to God, to society, and to the selves they are still becoming.

We are small on purpose. Fifteen students to a class, on average. Thirteen to one across the whole university. Four schools — Arts & Sciences, Business & Technology, Education, Nursing & Health Sciences — with forty-eight undergraduate concentrations, twelve master’s programs, and three doctorates between them.

Everything we do is informed by the CSFN charism: faithful listening, loving relationships, recognizing God in the everyday. That is not a slogan. It is how we answer the door when a student knocks.

Founding Year

1954

The year the Sisters of the Holy Family of Nazareth opened a small Catholic college in Northeast Philadelphia. Seventy-two years later, here we still are.
