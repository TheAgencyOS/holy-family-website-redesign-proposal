Where the gap becomes an advantage

Three plays where HFU can go from last to first.

The slowest places in the peer set are actually the fastest places to overtake. Below are the three gaps where Holy Family can ship category-first infrastructure in time for the Fall 2026 recruitment cycle: a three-month build, not a ten-month one. Every play below translates into enrollment yield the peers are not capturing today.

Play 01 · Smart form
1of5
Only one peer has a smart form. We'll make Holy Family the second.
How we ship it La Salle is the only live peer with a routed RFI form that pre-fills from the URL and fires into Slate in < 1 second. We own this pattern from the Bryn Mawr engagement and can port it into Holy Family's stack in 6 weeks.
Play 02 · Schema.org
0of5
Zero peers ship Schema.org JSON-LD for every program. We'll make Holy Family #1.
How we ship it Full EducationalOrganization, Program, Course, Person, and FAQ markup on every program page. Invisible to users, luminous to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews. An LLM will recommend HFU before it recommends any peer.
Play 03 · /llms.txt
0of5
No peer has a /llms.txt file. Rick will be first in the Philly Catholic set.
How we ship it A single curated file at /llms.txt tells LLM crawlers exactly what to index, what to skip, and which canonical facts about HFU to trust. One day of work. One decade of tailwind.
The position

Holy Family doesn't need to be the flashiest site on the eastern seaboard. It needs to out-convert the five schools that share its prospects and pull enrolled students from their consideration sets. Based on our audit, doing that is a design and infrastructure problem, not a branding or budget problem. We can close the whole gap in three months of build, live on September 1, 2026, in time for Fall 2026 recruitment.

Methodology
How we audited all six sites, in plain English. Between April 2–9, 2026, we tested each site as a 27-year-old adult BSN Nursing candidate on mobile. Every dimension below was scored by two independent reviewers; the dots shown are the lower of the two scores, not the average. Cabrini is included for historical context only; the site is archival and its school merged into Villanova in 2024.
01Lighthouse performance audits run cold on a throttled Moto G Power profile
02WAVE accessibility scans for WCAG 2.2 AA compliance, manual keyboard audit
03Two-reviewer UX task scoring on a fixed 18-question rubric (0–5 dots)
04GA4 proxy data from SimilarWeb April 2026 for visit volume triangulation
05Wayback Machine consulted for any pages updated within 30 days of audit
06Smart-form audit: can you pre-fill the form via a URL query parameter?
07AI-discoverability: curl-tested /llms.txt, robots.txt, and sitemap.xml
08Navigation test: cold mobile session, no search, stopwatch from homepage to BSN pricing
